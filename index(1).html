Delhi Student Support

Help, safety info, and a place to coordinate — for anyone caught up in today's situation, regardless of where you stand.

🚑 If you're hurt right now

  • All-India emergency number: 112 — police, fire, ambulance, works from any phone
  • Ambulance direct: 102 or 108
  • Major Delhi trauma centres: AIIMS Trauma Centre (Ring Road), Safdarjung Hospital, RML Hospital — all have 24/7 emergency wards and won't turn away an emergency case regardless of ability to pay
  • Women's helpline: 1091
If you're not sure where the nearest hospital is, tell 112 your location — they will direct you or dispatch help.

😮‍💨 Tear gas or pepper spray exposure

  • Move to fresh air / higher ground as calmly as possible — don't run through the cloud, walk crosswind out of it
  • Don't rub your eyes. Blink and let tears flush it out, then rinse with clean water or saline if available
  • Remove contact lenses if you can do so safely
  • Rinse exposed skin with cool water and mild soap; avoid oil-based lotions which can trap the irritant
  • Symptoms usually ease within 15–30 minutes. Trouble breathing, chest pain, or symptoms that don't improve → get medical help

⚖️ Legal help / if someone is detained

  • NALSA free legal aid helpline (toll-free, govt-run): 15100
  • Delhi State Legal Services Authority (DSLSA) — provides free lawyers and is the fastest official route if someone has been detained
  • Human Rights Law Network (HRLN) and PUCL Delhi have historically provided pro-bono legal support during protests — search their current official contact pages, as helpline details change
  • If someone is detained: note the exact time, location, and police station if known. Under law, family/friends must be informed of the grounds of detention — ask clearly and calmly for this
Verify any legal aid contact through the organization's official website before relying on it — phone numbers for volunteer networks change often during fast-moving situations.

🧠 If you need to talk to someone

  • KIRAN mental health helpline (govt, 24/7, toll-free): 1800-599-0019
  • iCall psychosocial helpline: search "iCall TISS helpline" for current hours and number
